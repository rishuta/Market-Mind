# MarketMind Product Roadmap

Purpose: define the strategic evolution of MarketMind from its current state to its long-term vision.  
Source of truth: [MarketMind Documentation Index](00_INDEX.md), [MarketMind Product Blueprint](product-blueprint.md), [MarketMind Design Language](02_DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md), [MarketMind UI Guidelines](03_UI_GUIDELINES.md), and [MarketMind Feature Filter](04_FEATURE_FILTER.md).

This is a product roadmap, not a task list. It describes direction, sequence, and product value without implementation details.

# Vision

MarketMind becomes an AI financial advisor that helps users make better financial decisions every day.

The long-term goal is not to expose more market data. It is to help users understand what matters, decide what to do, and feel calmer about money decisions.

MarketMind should grow from analysis into guidance, and from guidance into an ongoing financial relationship.

# Product Evolution

## Phase 1: Stock Analysis Tool

MarketMind began by helping users answer a focused question:

> Should I buy this stock?

This phase established the foundation for explainable stock analysis, company review, market context, and verdict-based recommendations.

## Phase 2: Adaptive Investment Planner

MarketMind expanded from single-stock analysis into broader investment planning.

This phase helped users decide how much to invest, how much cash to hold, and how to allocate across major buckets such as index exposure, direct stocks, gold, crypto, and cash.

## Phase 3: AI Financial Advisor

MarketMind is now moving toward a full advisor experience.

This phase prioritizes decision-first pages, AI Daily Brief, clearer recommendation language, preference-aware planning, and explanations that help users understand why MarketMind recommends a path.

## Future: Personal Financial Operating System

The long-term opportunity is a personal financial operating system that supports portfolio health, goals, cash flow, tax awareness, retirement planning, and personalized coaching.

This future should still feel like MarketMind: calm, trustworthy, practical, and decision-first.

# Completed Milestones

- Adaptive Allocation Engine: MarketMind can recommend an allocation that responds to user profile, horizon, and current conditions.
- Dynamic Bucket Scoring: The product can compare major investment buckets and translate relative opportunity into a plan.
- Market Regime Detection: MarketMind can adapt advice based on whether the environment calls for confidence, caution, or protection.
- Sector Diversification: The planner can avoid overconcentration and encourage healthier exposure across market areas.
- Gold & Crypto Intelligence: Alternative exposures can be handled with risk-aware guidance instead of generic inclusion.
- Preference-Based Investing: Users can express preferences while MarketMind preserves advisor judgment and safety constraints.
- Product Blueprint: The product now has a clear constitution for what MarketMind is and is not.
- Design Language: The experience now has a defined emotional, visual, and communication philosophy.
- UI Guidelines: Page responsibilities and decision-first screen structures are now documented.
- Feature Filter: New ideas can be judged before they create feature creep.

# Current Sprint

## MarketMind V2 Experience

The current sprint focuses on turning MarketMind into a clearer, calmer, more advisor-like product experience.

Goals:

- AI Daily Brief
- Invest redesign
- Market Overview
- Analyze redesign
- Design system implementation

Success means users can understand what MarketMind recommends faster, with less interpretation and fewer technical distractions.

# Upcoming Milestones

## Milestone 1: AI Daily Brief

Purpose: Make Home the daily orientation layer for MarketMind.

User value: Users quickly understand what is happening today, what matters, and where to go next.

Success outcome: Home no longer duplicates Invest My Money. It becomes a concise daily advisor briefing.

## Milestone 2: Market Overview

Purpose: Replace Find Opportunities with a market context experience.

User value: Users understand market regime, risks, sectors, sentiment, commodities, and crypto context without mistaking the page for a direct buy list.

Success outcome: Market Overview helps users interpret the environment while Invest remains the place for allocation decisions.

## Milestone 3: Portfolio Intelligence

Purpose: Help users understand whether their current portfolio is healthy.

User value: Users get practical insight into concentration, risk, diversification, cash, and rebalancing needs.

Success outcome: Portfolio answers "Am I on track?" before showing detailed holdings.

## Milestone 4: Goal-Based Investing

Purpose: Connect recommendations to user goals and time horizons.

User value: Users can understand how today's decisions support specific goals such as wealth building, stability, or long-term planning.

Success outcome: MarketMind recommendations feel more personal without becoming more complex.

## Milestone 5: Financial Health Score

Purpose: Summarize the user's overall financial position in a clear advisory signal.

User value: Users can see whether they are financially balanced, overexposed, underinvested, or holding too little cash.

Success outcome: The score points to the next best action instead of becoming another raw metric.

# Future Vision

Potential long-term directions include:

- AI Financial Coach
- Retirement Planning
- Tax Insights
- Portfolio Optimization
- Multi-account support
- Broker integrations
- Learning from user preferences
- Personalized financial guidance

These are future opportunities, not commitments. Each should pass the feature filter before being planned.

Long-term expansion should make MarketMind more useful without weakening its advisor identity.

# Product Philosophy

MarketMind succeeds by helping users make better decisions, not by exposing more data.

Every milestone should move the product closer to becoming a trusted AI financial advisor. A roadmap item earns its place when it reduces uncertainty, makes the next action clearer, and supports the user's long-term financial confidence.

The best roadmap is not the one with the most features. It is the one that builds the most trust with the least unnecessary complexity.
